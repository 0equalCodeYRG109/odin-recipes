Jalebi

Description

Mawa Jalebi is a dessert that's very popular in North Indian cuisine and can be easily made at home. If you are a fan of jalebi and it tops the list of you favourite sweets, you must try Mawa Jalebi to relish a different rendition of your favourite sweet dish. Made with very simple ingredients like maida, khoya and milk, this sweet dish is an absolutely irresistible and is worth trying out. The texture of this dessert is crunchy as well as soft due to the perfect mixture of khoya and milk. The crispy surface of this sweet delight is sure to make you go-wow! Traditionally, jalebis are made in spiral form which are deep fried in refined oil. It can be paired up with vanilla ice creams or rabri too! Be it a normal get-together or a lavish party, this dessert will surely win hearts. Serve it in your next kitty party, potluck or as a snack on a winter evening and amaze your guests with your culinary skills!

Ingredients

  • 200 grams khoya
  • 300 grams sugar
  • 1 tablespoon ghee
  • 50 grams all purpose flour
  • 16 saffron (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare batter : To prepare this delicious dessert recipe, take a large bowl and add all-purpose flour in it. Next, add a little amount of water and stir until all lumps get dissolved. Add some more water and make a batter of thin consistency. Whisk the batter for 4-5 minutes, cover and keep it aside for an hour on a warm place.
  2. Prepare maida-mawa mixture : In the next step, add 2-3 tablespoons of milk in crumbled mawa and mash until it becomes soft. Cover and keep it aside for an hour as well. After 1-hour, mix mawa and maida mixture and whisk until all ingredients blend nicely. Mixture for making mawa jalebi is ready.
  3. Prepare sugar syrup : Now, dissolve the saffron in 1 tablespoon of water in a small bowl. Next, put a pan on medium flame and add sugar in it along with cup water into it. Cook for 2-3 minutes more once the sugar dissolves completely. Check the syrup for its consistency. When the sugar is ready, add saffron water into it and turn off the flame.
  4. Fry jalebis : Next, heat ghee in a pan with a heavy bottom or base. Fill up the jalebi mixture in the muslin cloth, tying it completely and making a small hole at the bottom. When the ghee is hot enough, make small jalebis in the form of a spiral. Make 3-4 jalebis or as many as possible in the pan. Fry until they turn golden brown in colour and take them out in a plate.
  5. Soak in sugar syrup : Soak these jalebis in sugar syrup for 2 minutes and then take them out in some other plate. Likewise, prepare all jalebis. Serve the Mawa Jalebi hot with rabdi or kheer.
